The Corporeal Beast is one of the most iconic bosses in Runescape, having held the title of highest combat level for a very long time. Back in 2008, it was without a doubt one of the most challenging bosses in the game. We've come a long way since then, though, and Corp is no longer the ferocious menace it used to be. We find ourselves in a tragic meta where this mighty foe is reduced to a chicken in solos, or gets effortlessly picked apart in 1+6 (or more) altscape, with no input from other players required whatsoever. There is one saving grace for this boss which is the fact that it is very accessible to large groups and as such is still an integral part of different clans' bossing activities. With that said, I've decided to give it my best to redesign this boss with the following guidelines in mind: Maintain the integrity of the original. Reincorporate elements of the fight where possible. Revitalize the meta to encourage its intended audience - large groups and masses. However, it should still be viable in smaller scales. Spec pool meta and excessive altscaping should be heavily disincentivized. Keep it low budget. Assume minimal support from the art department and reuse assets where possible (similar to PNM/Hard Mode ToB). I hope you enjoy this read. Feedback is always appreciated! ❤️

Up to 75 players can fight the Corporeal Beast at a time. The fight setup system functions much like the Nightmare fight does, with players being able to join the area within a 30-second period before the boss spawns. This is the same period for the boss' respawn timer. Once the fight begins, players are unable to enter the arena and must wait outside until either the Beast or the players emerge victorious.

Currently, the Corporeal Beast only ever rolls loot for one player, regardless of however many people are involved in the fight. This makes massing the boss far less profitable than soloing, or soloing with a bunch of alts. To make Corp more appealing to do in larger groups, drops will scale with party size and, as alluded to with the HP scaling, kills/hr will favor these groups as well. Current Gp/hr for different scales and methods have been taken into consideration for these changes.

The Corporeal Beast rolls loot for the player who dealt the most damage. There is a 1/585 chance that the loot roll hits the sigil drop table, for which there is a 3/7 chance to receive an Arcane sigil, a 3/7 chance for the Spectral sigil, and a 1/7 chance for the Elysian sigil. (This is how the current system works) For each additional player past the first, there is an incrementing 50% chance that they will also roll for loot. For example:
1️⃣ Solo: 100% 2️⃣ Duo: 100% + 50% 3️⃣ Trio: 100% + 100% (2 players are guaranteed to receive loot, the third will not receive loot) 4️⃣ 4-man: 100% + 100% + 50% (2 players are guaranteed to receive loot, either the third or fourth may receive loot, but not both) 5️⃣ 5-man: 100% + 100% + 100% (3 players are guaranteed to receive loot, the other 2 will not receive loot) And so forth.
Over time, the GP/kill will even out to: Base kill GP + (Base kill GP * 0.5 * (Players - 1))

The Corporeal Beast chases the nearest player, and attempts to use basic crush-based melee attacks every 4 ticks. This attack can deal up to 33 damage, and is highly accurate (~84.5% accuracy against full Justiciar armor), but can be negated entirely with Protect from Melee. Every 7 ticks, the Corporeal Beast checks if any players are standing underneath it. If there are, it will perform a Stomp attack that always deals 30-51 damage. The Beast also checks for the nearest player on the same 7-tick interval.

After falling below 90% HP, the Beast will perform the Rampage special attack either on its next melee attack, or after 12 ticks have passed (whichever comes first). ⏲️ This attack always has a duration of 32 ticks. When Rampaging, the Beast performs a stomp animation and continues to chase the nearest player, but instead uses its powerful Stomp attack every 3 ticks, dealing damage to anyone underneath it and within its melee distance (7x7 AoE). Additionally, boulders periodically fall from the ceiling of the cavern every 4 ticks. This happens for a total of 8 cycles, over the course of the Rampage. There is a 3-tick indicator shadow before landing, meaning that a new set of boulders fall each time the previous one hits the ground. A total of 16 boulders fall per cycle, on any random tiles of the room. They do not target players. They have a 3x3 AoE, dealing 18-28 damage to players standing on its central tile, and 12-18 damage to players on its adjacent tiles. Protect from Missiles reduces the total damage by 50%, down to 9-14 and 6-9 damage respectively.

At 50% HP, the Beast becomes immune to damage and walks to the center of the room, pushing away any players in the center. Note that it does not fall below 50% HP. If the Beast was Rampaging when it hit this threshhold, it will cancel out of it and boulders will stop spawning (though the current set will still fall and deal damage). Upon reaching the center, the Corporeal Beast howls and summons Spirits from each of the 5 graves scattered in the cave. The exact number of Spirits spawned depends on the scale of the fight. Spirits Scaling:
1-5 Players: 5 Spirits, 1 per grave 6-24 Players: 1 additional Spirit per player, each one spawning in clockwise order of graves, starting with NW. 25+ Players: 25 Spirits, 5 per grave
After spawning, the Spirits will wander to a random position within 6 ticks. In this time, up to 3 Spirits may recite one of a few lines of dialogue: > "Save us!" > "Help us..." > "End this misery." > "No more suffering." > "We can never go home..." > "Where is my son?" > "Mother?" > "Why can I not remember?" > "She will devour us all!" > "AAAAAAAAH!" This has no effect on gameplay and serves only to give some flavor to the fight.

The afflicted player will repeatedly take typeless damage from the Core every 4 ticks, increasing over time. Each time the Core does 4 instances of damage (every 16 ticks), it will increase its damage output.
First 4 instances (16 total ticks / 9.6 seconds): 2 damage Next 4 instances (32 total ticks / 19.2 seconds): 4 damage Next 4 instances (48 total ticks / 28.8 seconds): 7 damage Next 4 instances (64 total ticks / 38.4 seconds): 10 damage Next 4 instances (80 total ticks / 48 seconds): 14 damage All instances after this point deal 18 damage.
The Core continues to damage the infected player until they die or escape from the fight, after which it will target a new random player and repeat the process. While the player is infected, they will be unable to eat or drink any consumables they may have. Attempting to do so will result in the following chatbox message: "The dark energy core suppresses your digestive system." As such, the player is unable to use conventional methods to restore Hitpoints, Prayer and other stats, and Run energy. Non-consumable methods of restoration and stat boosting such as the use of Ancient or Lunar spells or the Redemption prayer remain unaffected. Furthermore, any previously active stat boosts or other effects like stamina boost will also remain unaffected. As soon as the player is infected, spirit vortexes will spawn at three different locations in the arena.
