The Wilderness Bosses were one of the earliest updates OSRS had, and it shows. The design philosophy of these bosses is much more akin to a traditional 2006 era Runescape than the modernized PvM we know today. Would these bosses have been released back then, I think I could genuinely imagine many people having fond memories of them like they do with KBD or Corp. But that is not the case, and what we're left with are team bosses that people fight solo by fundamentally breaking them into unintentional safespots, ignoring all their mechanics. In my attempt at reworking Callisto, I've opted to focus on creating a boss fight with lots of replayability. I do not want the fight to feel like the same 3 minutes repeated over and over. And most importantly, I want to focus on the relationship between PvMer and PvPer. In this fight, these two should feel more like adversaries than predator and prey. We could have a whole debate on the state of interaction between these two communities and what the Wilderness does for it, but that could be its own essay. Without further ado, let's get started!

The Zarosian Empire was responsible for some of the most admirable fortresses and temples Gielinor has ever seen. One such temple was Annakarl, located in the northern regions of Forinthry. To guard this temple, a watchtower was to be raised on a hill a few leagues south of it. However, with the fall of Zaros and the advent of the God Wars in the Third Age, Annakarl and all its kindred fortresses were brought under siege, and one by one, fell to the powers of both Saradomin and Zamorak. The attack on Annakarl could better be described as a massacre, as there were only a number of Zarosian priests defending the temple by the time the west of Forinthry had fallen. All of them were slaughtered, with no successful retaliation but for one wounded Zamorakian soldier. Among the corpses of the slain, they pillaged supplies, what little weapons they had, and a strange pendant, which their chieftain carried with him. The Zamorakian legion responsible for fall of Annakarl were tasked with the destruction of its watchtower as well, but when they arrived, they had found no watchtower, only a barren plateau upon a hill. There are no records of a watchtower having ever been built upon this hill, but the wounded Zamorakian soldier reported a massive storm brewing atop it. As he tried to climb the rockslide with his crippled body, he recorded in his journal only the screams of his comrades and an unearthly growl piercing his ears. Only a few other words could be discerned after we looted these pages from his torn limb: "He brought the winter and the winter took them".

Callisto introduces the concept of Adaptive Scaling to OSRS. In this case, this only applies to his Hitpoints, but could theoretically be used for any stat. Adaptive scaling means that the total HP (or stat) of a boss can change throughout the fight (not just at the start, like CoX or Nightmare), depending on the amount of players currently in the arena. It will increase if a player enters, and decrease if a player leaves. The total percentage of HP remains the same throughout the whole fight. Callisto scales by his Hitpoints by 1200 for each player in the arena. Example: Callisto has 1200 HP in a solo. The player soloes the boss down to 600 HP (50%). Another player enters the room, scaling the total HP up to 2400, and current HP to 1200 (50% of 2400). Both players continue fighting Callisto until he falls to 480 HP (20% of 2400), and the first player has to leave. Callisto now scales down to 240 HP (20% of 1200) in a solo.

At 80% HP, Callisto channels the inner power within him to call forth the winds of winter. From this point onwards until Callisto hits 20% HP, the arena will be permanently affected by 1 of 8 different environmental hazards. We will refer to these as "Arena Phases". Each of these lasts a little under a minute. The arena phase chosen is random each time (including the first one), but Callisto will not repeat the same one consecutively. They do not change the attacks that Callisto does himself.

That may have looked like a lot of things to take in at once, but in reality, you will only ever have to tackle 1 or 2 Arena Phases in a single kill. The Arena Phases introduce replayability to a boss fight that is fairly quick but grinded for a long time. Each Arena Phase lasts just under a minute, and fights are expected to take 2-3 minutes for most players. Only 60% of that fight (from 80-20% of Callisto's HP) will include Arena Phases, so most experienced players will only ever encounter 2 phases in a single fight. Some players would be able to only get 1 phase per kill. Each kill will feel new and different as there are 56 different versions of how a fight can play out with 2 arena phases, even more if you count the variations in each one. That being said, we're not quite done yet!
