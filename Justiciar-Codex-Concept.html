The Justiciar Codex is a new prayer book that would be introduced as a reward from the Grandmaster quest in the Darkmeyer series! (Note that some of the more powerful Prayers could be unlocked through more endgame content, as per the case with Rigour and Augury) It requires at least Level 55 Prayer to use. The book's central theme is the divine power bestowed by Saradomin onto his Justiciars. As an alternative prayer book, the Justiciar Codex's main goal is to provide a new approach to existing content. It features prayers that bolster both offensive and defensive capabilities, rather than focusing only on one, but in a very different way to what the normal prayer book offers. It will also feature the first team-oriented prayers! The Justiciar Codex, all prayers unlocked:

Much like the normal Prayer book, Prayers are divided into three categories: Overheads (Protect from Melee, Redemption, Smite, etc.) Boosts (Sharp Eye, Mystic Might, Piety, etc.) Miscellaneous (Protect Item, Rapid Heal, Preserve, etc.) The Justiciar Codex follows the same rules as the normal Prayer book. This means... You can only use 1 "overhead" type prayer at a time. You can only use 1 "boost" type prayer at a time. You may activate as many "miscellaneous" type prayers as you like. Let's take a moment to talk about the three Guard Prayers before we move on, because these are some of the key prayers of this book. So much content is balanced around the normal prayer book's 100% reduction Protection prayers, so there are a few ramifications that must be taken into account. The Basics: All damage reduced is rounded down, but will deal a minimum of 1 damage. (e.g. enemies that hit 2s would still hit 1s). Similarly, all damage increased (by the 150% modifier) is rounded down, with a minimum of +1 damage increase. (e.g. enemies that hit 1s would have a max hit of 2). Non-100% Reductions: There are certain monsters that still deal damage through Protection prayers, albeit at a reduced amount. Guard Prayers will reduce 75% of the base % reduction.
e.g. Protection Prayers reduce 50% of P3 Verzik's attacks -> Guard Prayers will reduce 37.5% (75% of 50) Protection Prayers reduce 75% of Olm's attacks -> Guard Prayers will reduce 56.25% (75% of 75)
The 150% damage modifier, on the other hand, applies after any other damage increasing effects.
e.g. The Nightmare deals 20% increased damage to players with the incorrect Protection prayer. The 150% damage amplifier is applied after this.
19:10
Hard Prayer Checks In most cases, prayer simply negates or reduces an amount of damage received. These can be considered "soft prayer checks." However, there are instances in which certain mechanics entirely depend on which Protection prayer you have active.
e.g. Jal-Ak (Inferno blob) reads your prayer on the 3rd tick, and uses a different attack on the 6th tick depending on what you prayed. Sotetseg's bouncing orbs will turn off your prayer if you are not praying the correct style.
These are what we will call "hard prayer checks". It is a certain mechanic that is not tied to the percentage of damage reduced, but rather depends entirely on which Prayer you are using. For the purposes of hard prayer checks, Guard prayers will act as both Protection prayers. Projectile Guard -> Protect from Magic + Protect from Missiles Physical Guard -> Protect from Missiles + Protect from Melee Spellblade Guard -> Protect from Magic + Protect from Melee (You can think of it as the Guard prayer internally having the logic for both Protection prayers, but at 75% reduction)
19:10
So, to use the examples from before, whilst using Guard prayers...
Jal-Ak (Inferno blob) reads your prayer on the 3rd tick, and uses a different attack on the 6th tick depending on what you prayed. If using Physical Guard, the blob reads the Ranged prayer present, and will attack with Magic. If using Spellblade Guard, the blob reads the Magic prayer present, and will attack with Ranged. With Projectile Guard, the blob reads both Ranged and Magic prayers, so its next attack will simply be 50/50 Magic or Ranged. Sotetseg's bouncing orbs will turn off your prayer if you are not praying the correct style. With Projectile Guard, players will never have their prayer turned off as they always pass the hard check with Ranged and Magic prayers. However, in spite of keeping their prayer up, the orbs will still deal 25% damage rather than negated entirely, because the damage taken is a soft prayer check.

75% Magic Reduction VS Dragons Lastly, the Protect from Magic prayer has special interactions with dragonfire. Naturally, the Projectile Guard and Spellblade Guards will also reduce dragonfire damage, but less so than Protect from Magic. Mage prayer against dragons could be considered a hard prayer check, but for the sake of balancing, it is going to be treated as its own special case. Refer to table of dragonfire protection: https://oldschool.runescape.wiki/w/Dragonfire If Mage prayer is effective against the dragon (all non-metallic dragons), the player will take 25% increased damage from the reduced value (with a minimum of +1 damage increase if it deals 1 or more damage). (Super) antifire potions and dragonfire shields are taken into account before the prayer, so if the player has both and the dragon's damage is reduced to 0, there is no difference between the Protection prayer and the Guard prayer. The next set of key prayers in the book - team prayers! Let's have a look at the interactions this has with other players. The Basics: The Blessing takes effect on all ticks that the prayer is active. A player must be within range of the Blessing player on the tick they attack (Wolf/Owl/Lion) to receive its effects. For Blessing of the Unicorn, the damage reduction is calculated when the enemy's attack animation occurs, not when taking damage. Players cannot receive the same Blessing from two different players, but they can receive different Blessings at the same time. (e.g. In a trio where one player uses Wolf and the other uses Owl, all players will receive the Melee and Ranged damage boost). Because players cannot receive the same Blessing multiple times, in the case of larger groups, the Blessing will always try to "find" players who are not currently blessed, provided they are within the area. The damage reduction provided by Blessing of the Unicorn rounds down, and reduces a minimum of 1 damage. DPS Comparisons Between Blessings and Classic Boost Prayers: These values are the difference between 1 player with a Blessing and the others using classic prayers, and everybody using classic prayers.

Finally, we come to the last set of key prayers which have been building up along the course of the whole prayer book. The Guards offer a new type of defensive prayers, the Blessings offer the first team prayers, and, to replace what you lose from Piety, Rigour, and Augury, you are given a new way to disintegrate your foes with the Holy Strike Prayers. The Basics: The player must only be interacting (red click) with the NPC to trigger the effect (even if the player is stunned or on weapon cooldown). Damage dealt (and Prayer points drained) is rounded down, but the player must have enough Prayer points to deal at least 1 damage for the effect to activate at all (7/4/3 for Glimmer/Light Ray/Holy Strike). All Holy Strike prayers have 10-tile (maximum) range. The attack's 20 tick cooldown is shared among all 3 Holy Strike prayers, and only counts down while one of these prayers is active. The attack will also instantly come off cooldown if the player is no longer in combat. Interactions with other prayers: Whilst using Noble Blood, Holy Strike prayers will still determine their damage from your current Prayer points, but drain that amount from your Hitpoints instead. Whilst using Zealotry, the strike will not drain any points if timed correctly with that prayer's effect. DPS Comparisons The Holy Glimmer and Holy Light Ray are slightly stronger than the 10% and 15% boost prayers respectively. As the Holy Beam Strike is the most powerful version of these prayers, we will only tackle a more in-depth DPS comparison for that.
19:11
Holy Beam Strikes range between 2-6% DPS increase over Piety and Rigour with max gear at enemies with 0 or Low Defence. This boost falls off when the Beam is delayed by 4-5 ticks per each Beam's 20 tick cooldown, or if the player is casting beams at <2/3rds of their maximum Prayer points. More notably, faster weapons like the Blowpipe benefit more from Rigour, so that is closer to the 6% DPS loss than weapons like the Twisted bow. Holy Beam Strikes range between 26-32% DPS increase over Piety with max melee at enemies with higher Defence, such as Tekton in Challenge Mode Raids or Kree'Arra. This is because Beams always deal a flat amount of true damage. In cases where the player cannot maximize their weapon's damage output (such as P2 Verzik, where players must lose 1 tick every 16 ticks), boost prayers naturally lose some of their DPS boost. However, Beams are activated upon red clicks, so they are not limited by this. When using Magic however, Beam Strikes are a near universal ~30% DPS increase. Augury only offers Magic accuracy, so Beams have a big advantage when it comes to raw damage. It is worth noting, however, that the effectiveness of many spells is dependent on their accuracy roll (e.g. Vulnerability or Ice Barrage), so Augury may be preferable where pure damage output isn't the main priority.
19:11
The Prayer drain rate of Holy Beam Strike at 99 Prayer is significantly higher than Piety/Rigour/Augury (40 per minute), at a staggering 170 points per minute. However, effective use of the Discipline, Perception and Sagehood prayers will allow the user to restore up to ~85 points per minute, which halves the amount required to upkeep Beam Strikes. (This value is naturally higher with Melee and Ranged than with Magic, but Magic also benefits higher DPS from the Beam Strikes).

The Justiciar Codex offers a new approach to playing existing content with completely new prayers. Guard Prayers: Defensive overheads that differ from the natural 100% protection prayers, forcing players to change up their strategies for many existing bosses. Blessing Prayers: Team-oriented prayers that significantly boost you and your teammates. Encourages grouping together and staying in sync! Holy Strike Prayers: A new way to deal massive amounts of damage beyond the standard damage percent increases. Introduces new kinds of miscellaneous effects not found anywhere else in the game. Bonus prayers that aim to synergize with the codex's key prayers.
