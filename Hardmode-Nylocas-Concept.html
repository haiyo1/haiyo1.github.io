Most of the enemies are exactly the same as the ones found in regular mode. They don't need more HP because you're still meant to deal with them quickly. In addition, pillar HP hasn't been touched at all. The sheer amount of nylos in here compared to normal mode should be enough to make them tougher to protect. I've introduced 2 new types of Nylos, and for simplicity's sake we'll refer to them as buffers (Nylocas Oruletos) and bombers (Nylocas Dynamenos).
The hard mode wave layout is inspired by, but fundamentally different from the regular mode layout. Functionally, it works identically to regular mode. There is a 4-tick cycle that is constantly present throughout the waves. A wave spawns at the start of this cycle (sometimes there are empty cycles where no waves spawn). If there are too many nylocas inside the room (surpassing the cap) when a new wave is meant to spawn, it will be staggered until the number of nylos in the room is beneath the cap. When the last nylocas dies, there is a 22-tick pause before the boss spawns. In addition, mechanical aspects of the nylocas (aggro range, explosion timer, attack speed, etc.) that we're already familiar with (melee/range/mage, smalls and bigs) are identical. I will cover the buffers and bombers when they appear in the waves. Key Differences: There are 42 waves, instead of 31. There are ~280 total nylos, instead of ~200. The waves are roughly 24 seconds longer. Although there are quite a few more waves, there are also fewer empty cycles. This means more nylos to deal with in a short period of time. The nylo cap has been changed to 20 and 40 rather than 12 and 24. This is necessary for the amount of nylos that will be coming in. The wave on which the cap increases is also different. Next I'll be covering the individual "segments" of the waves as well as introducing new mechanics and nylos.
Waves 1-5 The introduction to what's about to come. It starts almost identically to normal mode Nylocas, but with more aggressive variants. Big nylos also make their first appearance earlier on.
Waves 6-12 There is a 2-cycle gap between the last of the "intro" waves and the first chain wave. This is to allow the players to clear everything out just before they start spawning because there's a ton of nylos about to come in. Chains are predictable, but not exact spawns of double small nylo waves that come in quick succession. There are three sets of chains across the wave layout, and this is its first appearance. Rather than being the exact same spawns every time, chains will force players to adapt to situations. They are not completely random so it's not just a guessing game. Each set of nylos will either be same-colored, or two different colors. If same-colored, the next wave must necessarily be one nylo of the previous color + one of the other two colors, at random (the previous colored nylo will always be the unaggressive one). If they are different colors, the next wave must necessarily be a double of the same color of one of the two colors, at random. List of Possible Chains: Key: Melee Range Mage (edited)

Flashing nylos (the ones that change colors while travelling down the webs) are also introduced here.
Nylocas Oruletos (buffer) is a new type of Nylocas. Only the small variants (1x1) exist. They do not attack players nor pillars. Instead, they passively wander around the room. Their explosion timer is 26 ticks (this is half of every other nylo, which is 52). If this Nylocas is killed, all Nylocas within a 7x7 area of it will go into a Frenzy. A Frenzy will cause the Nylocas to attack every 2 ticks (rather than 3). In addition, pillar-focused Nylocas become aggressive to players. Note that natural death by explosion will not trigger a frenzy upon death.
Waves 13-17 These waves serve to introduce the buffers. Following the many waves of double nylos, and the smalls that spawn from the bigs, the buffers can serve as a rescue party for getting nylos off of pillars. They can also be a disaster and kill your entire team if the frenzy gets too many nylos.
After wave 17, webs will periodically fall onto the arena.
Webs are 1x1 entities which behave very similarly to the webs at Verzik Phase 3. 8 webs will fall onto random tiles of the room every 24 ticks. These have a 4 tick indicator before landing and becoming hazardous. The webs then remain on the floor for 12 ticks. Players who step onto a web will be rendered unable to move and attack until the web snaps or is destroyed. Players can attack webs to destroy them. They have 6 HP and no defensive bonuses. If a player is stuck on a web, 6 ticks will be added to its lifetime if it would otherwise have snapped before then. The webs deal 25-35 damage to players caught in them when they snap.
Example of a web attack layout.
Waves 18-25 A generous 3-cycle gap is given when webs start falling. Afterwards, another cycle of chain nylos is introduced. These will include flashing chains. Flashing colors for chains are predictable: > > > Loop The chain is always based off of the first set of colors. As with other flashing nylos, they will flash twice while travelling down the web. Example: First color: Second color: Third and final color: Next chain's first color: or (based off of the first color of the previous) The cap is also increased to 40 at wave 23. This is to account for the massive influx of new nylos coming in. Buffers are also periodically introduced to help(?) with pillar control. This segment of the waves is probably one of the steepest learning curves - introducing webs while handling with a large amount of flashing nylo chains.
Nylocas Dynamenos (bomber) is a new type of Nylocas. Only the big variants (2x2) exist. Upon spawning, they will run towards the center of the room (moving at 2 tiles per tick speed). If they successfully reach the center, they will pause for 1 tick before self-detonating into a massive explosion that damages all players in the room. The explosion deals reduced damage further away from the center. The path from spawn to center takes 8 ticks. As they cannot be frozen, destroying them quickly is the only way to prevent them from exploding. Despite being a "big nylo," they do not spawn small nylos upon death.
Explosion Damage:
0-1 tiles away from center: 70-90 2-3 tiles away from center: 50-70 4-5 tiles away from center: 30-50

Wave 26 is the first wave to introduce a bomber. This always spawns on the south lane. From this point onwards, all waves with big nylos have a chance of spawning a bomber in place of a big nylo. This chance increases the more waves that pass without one spawning:
First wave after a bomber spawn: 10% chance Second wave after a bomber spawn: 30% chance Third wave after a bomber spawn: 70% chance Fourth wave after a bomber spawn: Guaranteed bomber
(The chance resets each time a bomber spawns) Only one bomber nylo can spawn per wave. The lane it spawns from (and subsequently, the nylo it replaces) is random. Specifically, the waves on which a Bomber can spawn are:
26 (guaranteed) 27 28 29 31 33 35 37 39 42 (guaranteed)
Note that at least 4 bombers are guaranteed in any given raid, due to 2 guaranteed spawns and the fourth wave rule. Waves 26-29 serve as both an introduction to the bomber and a build-up to the finale of the waves. There are two 2-cycle gaps to allow players a moment to prepare themselves for the coming onslaught.
Waves 30-42 The finale of the waves, incorporating all previous segments of the fight together. Most of the nylo spawns are not aggressive to players. A generous amount of buffers are spawned to protect the pillars more effectively - as long as players can survive that. There are also continuous web spawns to handle as well as the task of keeping up with multiple flashing nylo chains. There are many opportunities for chinning/barraging if players are able to capitalize on them. And of course, the looming threat of a bomber every other wave helps build up the tension.
NOTE: The webs during the waves will stop spawning when the last nylo in the waves dies. Functionally, the fight is the same. The boss chooses a style for 10 ticks, does two attacks 4 ticks apart, and switches to another style at random. Vadnylocas Vasilias has identical stats to its regular mode counterpart. However, although his base damage does not change, the damage reduced by protection prayers does. Each of his attack styles have a special mechanic which determines the amount of damage players will take. Melee Attacks launch a web at each player's tile. The web travels for 3 ticks before landing and becoming hazardous. Like the ones in the waves, they remain on the ground for 12 ticks. If multiple players are standing on one tile, only one web will be launched to that tile (similarly to crystals spawning at Olm). Identically to normal mode, Protect from Melee completely negates damage from its attacks. Ranged Attacks will spread to all players within a 5x5 range (up to 2 tiles away) of the primary target. If an attack hits more than one player, Protect from Missiles will only reduce damage by 50% (max hit of 35). If an attack only hits the primary target, Protect from Missiles will reduce its damage by 75% (identically to normal mode). Magic Protection prayer reduces more damage based on distance away from the boss:
0-1 tiles away: 20% (max hit of 56) 2 tiles away: 40% (max hit of 42) 3 tiles away: 60% (max hit of 28) 4+ tiles away: 80% (max hit of 14)
 (edited)
At 60% HP, a Nylocas Matomenos will spawn from each lane (3 total). These behave identically to the ones found in Maiden. They will spawn and attempt to walk into the boss, healing it for 2x their remaining Hitpoints (though they do not increase the boss' damage). At 30% HP, another set of Nylocas Matomenos spawns, and Vadnylocas Vasilias' attack cycle is sped up. His two attacks will be 3 ticks apart, instead of 4. The time in between his last attack and the next color switch is reduced by 1 tick. This means he will switch colors every 8 ticks, instead of 10. (Keep in mind that the reaction time in between switching colors and attacking remains the same).
The waves are about 20 seconds longer. The waves will not be exactly the same every time (though most spawns will remain fixed). There are dangerous web tiles to watch out for for the majority of the battle. There are new buffer nylos that are a double-edged sword - they can help you protect pillars, but they can also rinse your brews or stack you out. There are new bomber nylos that force players to stop what they're doing and immediately focus all they have into killing it. The boss has new mechanics that encourage players to not only take into account their prayers and gear, but where they're standing as well. Healers and increased attack speed are introduced at the boss to give the fight a sense of progression. (edited)
