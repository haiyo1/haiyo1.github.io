The Butcher's basic ranged attacks (flies) are identical to Bloat's. The only difference is that they deal higher damage: 20-30 (reduced by 50% with Protect from Missiles). Note that the Ranged level increase is purely visual to indicate this (as Bloat's Ranged level is not indicative of his damage either).
The falling flesh (hands/feet) attack is nearly identical to Bloat's:
There is a 3-tick indicator shadow before the damage tick. They fall in intervals of 6 ticks when the Butcher is above 40% HP, and 4 ticks when below. They deal 25-50 damage. They will stun the player for the same duration. Falling flesh stops spawning when the Butcher goes down.
However, there are three notable changes: There will only be 10-12 spawns per set of falling flesh, rather than 14-16. Whenever a player dies, 1 additional spawn is added to each set (making it 10-12 + 1 for each fallen player). A little morbid? Every fourth set of falling flesh, one of the indicators (at random) is replaced with an orange glowing shadow. This will indicate where a Tuznylocas will spawn. A hand/foot does not fall here.
At the end of each of the four corridors in the room, there are 5 gaps in the wall - one for each tile of a corridor.
A visual mockup of the five gaps at the end of a corridor. When the Butcher is below 80% HP, cleavers will be thrown from two of the five gaps (at random) at 6-tick intervals. When the Butcher is below 40% HP, it is increased to three of the five gaps. The cleaver is a projectile that travels in a straight line across the corridor, and cannot be skipped over (identical to Sepulchre arrows). Each one deals 15-25 damage and travels at 3 tiles per tick speed. Because of the speed and intervals of the cleavers, a new set of cleavers will spawn every time a previous set reaches the end of its lifetime. However, there is a 3-tick indicator where the dangerous gaps will glow a bright red before firing cleavers.
A visual mockup of the dangerous gap indicator.
The cycles of cleavers (below 40% HP as there are three in each set) Note that unlike falling flesh, cleavers do not stop spawning during periods where the Butcher goes down. They are a constant hazard throughout the fight after the 80% HP threshhold is breached.
Tuznylocas are explosive variants of their Nylocas counterparts. They are similar to the exploding Nylocas found in Verzik P2, but have some fundamental differences. When the Butcher is below 90% HP, falling flesh begins to fall from the ceiling (same as Bloat). From this point onwards, a single Tuznylocas will spawn on every fourth set of falling flesh. It can either be Hagios or Toxobolos, at random. (A golden shadow indicator takes the place of the regular grey shadow).
Tuznylocas Hagios and Tuznylocas Toxobolos, respectively. Similarly to regular Nylocas, the Hagios variant can only be killed using magic, and the Toxobolos variant can only be killed using ranged. However, using an incorrect style against them will cause them to pause for 1 tick before immediately exploding in a 7x7 area. They will also explode if a player comes within a 2 tile range of them. Notwithstanding explosions, they do not attack players. If they are killed correctly, they do not explode at all. The explosion itself, similar to the exploding Nylocas at Verzik, deals decreasing damage based on distance further away from them:
On its tile: 64 damage 1 tile away: 48 damage 2 tiles away: 32 damage 3 tiles away: 16 damage 4+ tiles away: Safe
Tuznylocas have an aggro range of 5 tiles. Once they've targeted a player, they will attempt to "attack with melee" - although they will never attack, they'll simply explode when within 2 tiles of any player (does not have to be the targeted player). Before a player comes within its aggro range, they will simply wander slowly (moving only one tile in a random direction every 6 ticks). (edited)
Indication of the different damage values within the 7x7 explosion, as well as aggro range and explosion trigger range. Tuznylocas will self-detonate on the spot after 75 ticks (45 seconds). There can be multiple Tuznylocas in the room at once if they are not destroyed quickly. Note that, because the hands begin to fall more frequently when the Butcher is below 40% HP, Tuznylocas will consequently spawn more frequently as well. Trivia: "Tuz" means "Fire" in Hungarian.
The Butcher's movement cycle is nearly identical to Bloat's.
He moves at walking speed (1 tile per tick) when above 60% HP. He begins running (2 tiles per tick) when below 60% HP. He can turn at any point after 32 ticks of movement. He can go down at any point after 39 ticks of movement. If he turns, he will add +5 ticks before going down. When he goes down, he will remain stationary for 32 ticks (with the stomp attack occurring 30 ticks in).
The only difference is:
The Butcher will continue running for the rest of the fight when below 60% HP.
(Whereas Bloat can switch speeds when below 40% HP) However, while he is down, he has three brand new mechanics: flashing, glowing and rebound. As soon as the Butcher goes down, a 24-tick timer starts, which ends 2 ticks before the stomp animation begins. This timer is split up into 6 sets of 4-tick cycles. On the first tick of every cycle, the Butcher will flash red to indicate that a new cycle has started. We will call this a "reset."
The 24-tick timer, split into 6 sets of 4-tick cycles. If players damage the Butcher for 6% of his total HP or higher in a single cycle, he will glow red for the whole duration of the next cycle. E.g. 5 players must deal 180+ damage within 4 ticks (starting from the reset tick) to trigger a glow. If players do not attack the Butcher during a glowing cycle, he will stop glowing after the cycle ends and resume sleeping. If players attack the Butcher at all during a glowing cycle, he will rebound with a powerful attack. When a rebound occurs: A bright red tint covers all players' screens on the tick which he is attacked on, dealing 30-50 damage to all players. The Butcher immediately awakens from his slumber 2 ticks after being attacked (he begins the stomp animation). Note that if a rebound is triggered on the very last possible tick of the last cycle, the Butcher will awaken at the same time as usual. Otherwise, he will awaken prematurely, reducing the time window for getting in damage.
